Research

Undergraduate thesis Paper
Title: War and Work: Labor Market impacts of the Nepalese Civil War. [Replication and Paper Link]
Description: This paper studies the labor market consequences of Nepalese Civil War (1996 -2006 A.D.). Using two rounds of Nepal Labor Force Survey (NLFS) data, the paper uses Doubly Robust DiD (DrDid) to assess the effect of conflict intensity (as proxied by conflict deaths) on employment measures. Being in a conflict-intense region had minimal effect on the probability of being employed, although these regions saw reduced work hours.

Exogenous Variation in Family Size : Sex Composition of Children and the third birth. [Link]
Description: First paper as part of the course Applied Economics Research (Written as a third year undergraduate student). The paper looks at whether families whether families who had two girls as their first two children tended to conceive a third child more than those families who had two boys as their first two children in the context of Nepal.
