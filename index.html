    I am Suhishan Bhandari, a recent Economics undergraduate. I write about my interests here, which are mainly short projects on applied statistics using Bayesian Inference in R, Stan and Python. Below is a list of some of my writing and projects.

  • Premier League Match Predictions using Bayesian Hierarchical Models.

    The goal of this blog is to predict the final gameweek (gameweek 38) of the Premier League 2023/24 season. To accomplish this task: two models are used a) Complete Pooling model and b) Hierarchical Model.

  • How to use hurdle lognormal likelihood to model outcomes?

    The goal of this blog is to outline how, in response to natural constraints in a variable like work hours (the variable is always positive with lots of zeros), we can employ a hurdle log-normal model for likelihood instead of the usual normal distribution assumption.

  • Can Forest cover work as an instrument for conflict incidence?

    In this blog, I try and incorporate uncertainity into the well-used and establish relationship between Forest cover and conflict incidence in the Conflict Literature. This is done to see whether one can indeed use Forest Cover as a valid instrument, especially if analyzed with a causal design in mind.

  • Strategic Voting in Nepal's Local Level Mayoral Elections.

    What was the extent of competition and tactical voting in Nepal's two Local Level Mayoral Elections in 2017 and 2022? The blog estimates the Effective number of parties and the Divergenian Equlibria.

  • A Simple Political Agency and Accountability Model

    A simple R visualization of the math and model behind Political agency and accountability.
